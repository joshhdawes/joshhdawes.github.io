Welcome!

I lead the research activities in Runtime Verification in the SVV group of Interdisciplinary Centre for Security, Reliability and Trust (SnT) in Luxembourg, as part of the Horizon 2020 project COSMOS. I work with Professor Domenico Bianculli and Professor Lionel Briand.

SnT is a branch of the University of Luxembourg focused on R&D for industry partners.

I am also an External Expert at the CMS Experiment at CERN for work stemming from my PhD.

R&D

I work on static, dynamic and offline analysis of various kinds of computer systems.

With SnT, I lead the research on Runtime Verification as part of COSMOS, a Horizon 2020 project.

With SnT, I also do research on Log Analysis.

With CERN, I contribute to the development of the VyPR framework that I built while doing my PhD there.

A bit about my PhD - read my Thesis!

I completed my PhD with the University of Manchester while based at CERN on the Doctoral Student programme. I focused on Runtime Verification for performance analysis of programs, which involved developing:

  • A new specification language for source code level properties of programs.
  • Automated instrumentation and monitoring.
  • Offline analysis of monitoring results in order to explain property violations.
My supervisors were Dr Giles Reger (Manchester), Dr Giovanni Franzoni (CERN) and Dr Andreas Pfeiffer (CERN).

During my PhD, I led a small team in developing of the VyPR framework at CERN, whose underlying theory I developed for my PhD. My work on VyPR led to multiple CERN and CMS-funded projects, along with an inter-institute collaboration between CERN, the University of Manchester, and Universita della Svizzera italiana.
