Doctoral Student at CERN & Manchester

My CV.

I focus on program analysis, in particular the combination of static and dynamic analysis. The subject of my PhD has been how static and dynamic analysis can be combined in the form of Runtime Verification for performance analysis.

I lead development of the VyPR framework at CERN, whose underlying theory I developed for my PhD.

My work on VyPR over the past 3 years has so far led to multiple CERN and CMS-funded projects, along with an inter-institute collaboration between CERN, the University of Manchester, and Universit√† della Svizzera italiana.

My PhD has been supervised by Dr Giles Reger (Manchester), Dr Giovanni Franzoni (CERN) and Dr Andreas Pfeiffer (CERN)

News

July 2020 - Our tool demonstration paper on adding performance analysis to Continuous Integration processes (using VyPR) was accepted at the International Conference on Automated Software Engineering in Melbourne, Australia. This marks the first publication from the CERN-Manchester-USI collaboration.

July 2020 - Our VyPR tutorial proposal was accepted for presentation at the International Conference on Runtime Verification in Los Angeles, USA.

March 2020 - Marta Han from the University of Zagreb joined CERN as a Technical Student to work on VyPR's analysis tools.

November 2019 - Omar Javed from USI joined the CMS Experiment to work on a project based on VyPR.
