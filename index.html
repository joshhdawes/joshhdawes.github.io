Welcome! Bienvenue!

I am a Research Associate at the Interdisciplinary Centre for Security, Reliability and Trust in Luxembourg, working with Professor Domenico Bianculli and Professor Lionel Briand.

I am also an External Expert for work stemming from my PhD at the CMS Experiment at CERN in Geneva, Switzerland.

I completed my PhD with the University of Manchester while based at CERN. I focused on Runtime Verification for performance analysis of programs. My supervisors were Dr Giles Reger (Manchester), Dr Giovanni Franzoni and Dr Andreas Pfeiffer (CERN).

During my PhD, I led a small team in developing of the VyPR framework at CERN, whose underlying theory I developed for my PhD. My work on VyPR led to multiple CERN and CMS-funded projects, along with an inter-institute collaboration between CERN, the University of Manchester, and Universita della Svizzera italiana.

Research

I spend most of my time working on static, dynamic and offline analysis of various kinds of computer systems. I care about identifying problems that are being faced in practice, developing the theory to solve them, and building systems that are strong enough to be used in the real-world.

With SnT, I do research on Runtime Verification and Security as part of COSMOS, a Horizon 2020 project.

With SnT, I also do research on Log Analysis.

With CERN, I contribute to the development of the VyPR framework that I built while doing my PhD there.
